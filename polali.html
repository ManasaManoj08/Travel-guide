Polali Rajarajeshwari Temple

Polali Rajarajeshwari Temple is a significant Hindu temple located in Polali, near Mangalore in Karnataka, India. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Rajarajeshwari, a form of the Divine Mother. The story behind the Polali Temple is associated with a historical legend.
Legend has it that the temple was originally established by King Suratha, who was a great devotee of Goddess Rajarajeshwari. The king was childless and prayed fervently to the goddess for an heir. Pleased with his devotion, the goddess appeared before him and granted his wish. In gratitude, King Suratha decided to build a temple dedicated to Goddess Rajarajeshwari at the location where she had blessed him.
The temple was constructed in the Chola architectural style, and the presiding deity, Goddess Rajarajeshwari, is enshrined in a sanctum sanctorum. The main idol of the goddess is made of clay and is believed to be a Swayambhu, meaning self-manifested.
The uniqueness of the Polali Temple lies in the fact that it is one of the rare temples where the goddess is worshipped in a seated posture rather than the more common standing or dancing form. The goddess is depicted with eight arms, holding various weapons and symbols.
Another significant aspect of the Polali Temple is the annual festival known as 'Polali Rajarajeshwari Jatra Mahotsava.' This festival, held during the month of March or April, is a grand celebration that attracts a large number of devotees. The highlight of the festival is the 'Kodi Yetttu' ritual, where a ceremonial flag is hoisted to mark the commencement of the festivities.
During the festival, the temple hosts various cultural events, rituals, and processions. Devotees from different parts of Karnataka visit the Polali Temple to seek the blessings of Goddess Rajarajeshwari. The Polali Rajarajeshwari Temple stands as a symbol of devotion, tradition, and cultural heritage in the region, drawing worshippers and visitors alike.
