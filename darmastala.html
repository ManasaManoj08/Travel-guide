Dharmasthala Temple

The Dharmasthala Manjunatha Temple is a significant and renowned Hindu temple located in the town of Dharmasthala in Karnataka, India. The temple is dedicated to Lord Manjunatha (a form of Lord Shiva), and it is known for its unique tradition of charity and communal harmony. Here's the story behind the Dharmasthala Temple:
The origin of the Dharmasthala Temple is associated with the legend of the Jain chieftain, Birmanna Pergade, who lived in the region during the 16th century. According to the legend:
- Birmanna Pergade was a devoted follower of the Jain faith and a landowner in the region. One day, he found two divine-looking men (who were believed to be Shiva and Parvati in disguise) in his cattle pen.
- The divine couple requested him to provide them with a place to stay. Birmanna Pergade offered his hospitality and built a small shrine for them in the forest. Later, he had a dream in which Lord Shiva instructed him to build a temple in his name at Dharmasthala.
- Following the divine command, Birmanna Pergade constructed the Manjunatha Temple in Dharmasthala, dedicated to Lord Manjunatha (Shiva). The temple was established as a place of worship for people of all faiths and backgrounds.
Dharmasthala is well-known for its tradition of "Annadana" or free distribution of food to pilgrims and visitors. The temple's charitable activities extend to providing free meals, shelter, and medical facilities to those in need. The administration of the temple is managed by the Heggade family, who have been the hereditary trustees for generations.
The Heggade family is responsible for the maintenance of the temple and the implementation of the charitable activities. The concept of "Dharma" (righteousness) is central to the temple's philosophy, emphasizing the importance of selfless service and compassion towards others.
The Dharmasthala Temple stands as a symbol of religious harmony, charity, and communal coexistence. Pilgrims and visitors from different parts of the country visit the temple to seek the blessings of Lord Manjunatha and to witness the unique tradition of selfless service.
