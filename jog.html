Jog Falls

Jog Falls, located near Mangalore in the Shimoga district of Karnataka, India, is a breathtaking natural spectacle that captivates visitors with its sheer beauty and raw power. Nestled in the lush Western Ghats, Jog Falls is one of the highest plunge waterfalls in the country.
The falls, also known as Gerosoppa Falls or Jogada Gundi, are formed by the Sharavathi River plummeting from a height of about 830 feet (253 meters) in a single unbroken cascade. The mesmerizing display of water gushing down the rugged cliffs creates a mesmerizing misty spectacle, especially during the monsoon season when the flow is at its peak.
Surrounded by verdant forests and steep hills, Jog Falls offers a picturesque and serene setting. Visitors can witness the falls from different viewpoints, each providing a unique perspective of this natural wonder. The four cascades—Raja, Roarer, Rocket, and Rani—add to the grandeur of the falls, creating a stunning panorama.
Jog Falls is not only a visual treat but also a destination for nature enthusiasts and photographers. The roar of the falls, the lush greenery, and the cool mist in the air make it a memorable experience for those seeking a rendezvous with the raw power and beauty of nature in the vicinity of Mangalore.
