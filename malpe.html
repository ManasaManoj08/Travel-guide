Malpe Beach and St. Mary's Island

Malpe Beach and St. Mary's Island: Malpe is a picturesque beach town located near Udupi in Karnataka, India. It is known for its beautiful beaches and is a popular tourist destination. St. Mary's Island, a group of small islands off the coast of Malpe, adds to the charm of the region. Here's a brief overview of the story associated with Malpe and St. Mary's Island:
Malpe Beach:
1. Natural Beauty: Malpe is renowned for its pristine beaches, golden sands, and clear blue waters. The beach is surrounded by coconut palms, creating a tranquil and scenic environment.
2. Fishing Community: Malpe has been a significant fishing port for centuries, and it is home to a vibrant fishing community. Visitors to Malpe often get the chance to witness the activities of local fishermen and explore the bustling fish market.
3. Daria Bahadurgad Fort: Located on a small island near Malpe, the Daria Bahadurgad Fort adds a historical touch to the region. The fort was built by Basavappa Nayaka of Bidanur in the 18th century.
St. Mary's Island:
1. Geological Wonders: St. Mary's Island is a group of four small islands known for its unique geological formations, particularly the hexagonal basaltic rock columns. These formations are a result of volcanic activity and weathering over time.
2. Vascodagama's Visit: It is believed that St. Mary's Island was discovered by the Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama during his voyage to India in the late 15th century. The islands are said to have been named after Vasco da Gama's benefactor, Dona Maria.
3. Columnar Basaltic Lava: The distinctive hexagonal columns of basaltic lava on the islands are a natural wonder and a major attraction for visitors. The formations resemble giant stepping stones.
4. Boat Rides: Tourists can take short boat rides from Malpe Beach to reach St. Mary's Island and explore its unique geological features. The boat journey itself offers scenic views of the coastline.
While there may not be a specific myth or legend associated with Malpe and St. Mary's Island, their beauty, geological wonders, and historical connections make them popular destinations for both nature lovers and history enthusiasts visiting the Karnataka coast.
