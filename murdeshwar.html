Murdeshwar Shiva Temple

Murudeshwar Temple is a popular pilgrimage site located in the town of Murudeshwar in the Uttara Kannada district of Karnataka, India. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is situated on the coast of the Arabian Sea. The temple complex is known for its impressive architecture and the second-tallest Shiva statue in the world.
The origin of the temple is linked to the Hindu epic Ramayana. According to the legend, Ravana, the demon king of Lanka, was carrying the Atma Linga (Soul of Shiva) to his capital. Lord Ganesha, in the disguise of a Brahmin boy, tricked Ravana into placing the Atma Linga on the ground. Ravana, realizing the deception, tried to uproot it, and in the process, a piece of the Linga broke off and fell at the site of the current Murudeshwar Temple.
The Murudeshwar Temple as it stands today was built in the 16th century by a local businessman and philanthropist named R. N. Shetty. The temple underwent significant renovations and expansions in the 20th century, making it the grand structure that visitors see today.
The Maha Shivaratri festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm at the Murudeshwar Temple, attracting a large number of devotees.
The Murudeshwar Temple is not only a religious site but also a significant tourist destination, drawing visitors with its architectural marvels and scenic location by the sea.
