Varanga Lake Jain Temple

Varanga is known for its unique and ancient temple architecture, specifically the Kere Basadi (Lake Temple) dedicated to Lord Neminatha, a revered figure in Jainism. Here's a brief overview of the Varanga Kere Basadi and its story:
The legend associated with the Varanga Kere Basadi traces its roots to Jainism, an ancient Indian religion. Neminatha, the 22nd Tirthankara in Jainism, is believed to have a connection to this temple.
- Neminatha was a prince who renounced his kingdom to attain spiritual enlightenment. He is often associated with compassion and non-violence.
- The story goes that Lord Neminatha was married to Rajamati, and they had a grand wedding in Varanga. However, Neminatha decided to renounce his worldly life and chose the path of asceticism.
- It is believed that the Varanga Kere Basadi was built to commemorate the marriage of Lord Neminatha in Varanga.
The Varanga Kere Basadi stands as a testimony to the rich cultural and religious heritage of Jainism in Karnataka. The serene surroundings, coupled with the historical and religious significance, make it a place of interest for those interested in exploring the ancient temples of South India.
