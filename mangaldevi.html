Mangaladevi Temple

The Mangaladevi Temple in Mangalore, Karnataka, India, is a historic temple dedicated to the goddess Mangaladevi. The temple is considered one of the city's oldest and is believed to be the namesake of Mangalore. The story behind the Mangaladevi Temple is steeped in mythology.
According to local legend, the town of Mangalore got its name from a princess named Parimala, who was also known as Mangale. Parimala was a devoted worshipper of the goddess Mangaladevi. In another version of the legend, it is said that the town was named Mangalore after the presiding deity, Mangaladevi.
The temple's history dates back to the 9th century AD. It is believed that a princess from the Malabar region named Parimala (or Mangale) came to the region and performed penance to please the goddess Mangaladevi. Pleased with her devotion, the goddess appeared before her and granted her a boon.
As per the goddess's instructions, Parimala found a Shiva Linga and installed it on the banks of the Netravati River, where she had performed her penance. She established a shrine for the goddess Mangaladevi at this location. The temple became a place of worship and gradually evolved into the city that is now known as Mangalore.
The architecture of the Mangaladevi Temple reflects a blend of Kerala and Dravidian styles. The temple is situated at Bolar in the heart of Mangalore, near the confluence of the Netravati and Gurupura rivers.
The temple celebrates the Mangaladevi Rathotsava, a grand chariot festival, which attracts a large number of devotees and visitors. During this festival, the goddess is taken out in a beautifully decorated chariot, and various rituals and cultural events are held.
The Mangaladevi Temple holds cultural and historical significance in Mangalore and continues to be a revered place of worship for devotees seeking the blessings of the goddess Mangaladevi. The temple also attracts tourists interested in exploring the city's rich cultural and religious heritage.
