Halebidu and Bellur

Halebidu is a historical site in Karnataka, known for the Hoysaleswara Temple, while Bellur is likely a reference to Belur, another significant historical site in Karnataka, known for the Chennakesava Temple. Halebidu (Hoysaleswara Temple): Halebidu was the capital of the Hoysala Empire in the 12th century, and the Hoysaleswara Temple is one of the most outstanding examples of Hoysala architecture. Here's a brief overview:
The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva (Hoysaleswara) and was commissioned by King Vishnuvardhana in the 12th century to commemorate his victory over the Cholas. The legend suggests that the construction was started by the king and continued by his grandson Veera Ballala II. The Hoysaleswara Temple is known for its intricate carvings and sculptures that depict various mythological stories, deities, animals, and scenes from daily life. The temple complex also includes a smaller shrine dedicated to Hoysaleswara's consort, Shantaleswara.
Belur (Chennakesava Temple): The Chennakesava Temple in Belur was commissioned by King Vishnuvardhana in the 12th century. According to legend, the king was inspired by a saint named Ramanuja to build the temple. The Chennakesava Temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu in the form of Chennakesava. It is renowned for its intricate carvings, especially the famous bracket figures (Madanikas), which depict various dance poses and expressions. The temple is a masterpiece of Hoysala architecture. Both Halebidu and Belur, along with the nearby town of Shravanabelagola, form the "Golden Triangle" of historical and architectural wonders in Karnataka. These temples are not only significant for their religious and cultural importance but also for their architectural brilliance, showcasing the artistic skills of the Hoysala dynasty during the medieval period in South India.
