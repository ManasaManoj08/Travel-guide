Shree Sharavu Mahaganapathi Temple

The Shree Sharavu Mahaganapathi Temple is a well-known Hindu temple located in Mangalore, Karnataka, India. The temple is dedicated to Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed deity, and has a rich history associated with its establishment and worship.
The origin of the Shree Sharavu Mahaganapathi Temple is attributed to the divine vision of a saint named Shree Devendra Swamiji. According to legend, Swamiji had a dream in which he saw a divine light emanating from the ground in the location where the temple is currently situated. Believing it to be a divine message, Swamiji decided to build a temple at that very spot .
In 1920, under the guidance and inspiration of Shree Devendra Swamiji, the Sharavu Mahaganapathi Temple was established. The temple was constructed in the traditional South Indian architectural style, and the presiding deity is Lord Ganesha, also known as Mahaganapathi.
The Shree Sharavu Mahaganapathi Temple has become a significant religious and cultural center in Mangalore, attracting devotees and visitors alike. The temple not only serves as a place of worship but also contributes to various philanthropic activities and cultural events, making it an integral part of the community.
