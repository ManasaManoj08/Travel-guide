Kadri Manjunath Temple

Kadri Manjunath Temple, located in Mangalore, Karnataka, India, is one of the oldest and most revered temples in the region. The temple is dedicated to Lord Manjunatha, an incarnation of Lord Shiva.
The story behind the Kadri Manjunath Temple is associated with the legend of the sage Kadri, after whom the temple is named. According to the legend, there was a cave on the Kadri hills where a sage named Kadri used to perform intense penance and worship Lord Shiva. Pleased with his devotion, Lord Shiva appeared before him and granted him a boon. The sage requested Lord Shiva to stay in the form of a lingam (an iconic representation of the deity) in the Kadri region so that people could worship him.
Lord Shiva granted the sage's wish and manifested in the form of a lingam. The lingam is known as Manjunatha, which means "charming lord." The unique feature of this lingam is that it is said to be self-manifested or Swayambhu, meaning it was not sculpted by human hands.
As the story goes, over the centuries, the temple underwent various renovations and expansions. It has been influenced by various dynasties and rulers who contributed to its architectural development. The temple has a distinctive South Indian architectural style.
The Kadri Manjunath Temple is not only a place of worship but also an important cultural and historical landmark in Mangalore. The temple complex includes several other shrines dedicated to different deities, and it is surrounded by beautiful gardens and ponds. The temple hosts various festivals and celebrations throughout the year, attracting devotees and tourists alike.
The annual festival of the temple, known as Kadri Kambla, is a unique and traditional event featuring buffalo races. Devotees believe that participating in or witnessing the Kambla brings blessings from Lord Manjunatha. The Kadri Manjunath Temple stands as a symbol of faith, spirituality, and the rich cultural heritage of the region.
